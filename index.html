THE JUSTICE
PROTOCOL

The blockchain ushers a world of contracts enforced by code.

Smart contracts are smart enough to self-execute as programmed, but not to evaluate complex information or to render subjective judgments.

Human involvement is necessary when a dispute arises, when circumstances have drastically changed since the moment the contract was signed and when a high stakes decision must be made.

Kleroterion is a decision making protocol which uses the blockchain, crowdsourcing and game theory to settle disputes and to modify or revoke smart contracts in a fast, transparent and inexpensive way.

HOW IT WORKS?

Contract

Users create a smart contract and select Kleroterion as arbitration protocol

Dispute

A dispute arises, and the relevant information is sent to Kleroterion

Jury

A jury is drawn from the crowd. Jurors evaluate evidence and vote a decision.

Decision

The decision is enforced by the smart contract.

APPLICATIONS

Kleroterion is a protocol for fast, inexpensive, honest and transparent justice.

An entrepreneur hires a freelancer for a software development gig.
A dispute arises about the scope of the contract.

Should the freelancer give the money back to his client?

A jury of software experts analyze the evidence and votes a verdict.
A smart contract transfers the money to the winning party.

FRENQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION

Why is the protocol called Kleroterion?

Ancient Athenians developed an elaborate methodology for selecting representatives for public office and jurors for trials. Selection was made randomly with an allotment machine called Kleroterion. In Greek, Kleros means “chance”. An approximate translation is “the chance machine”. Expert selection in the Kleroterion protocol is loosely inspired in Greek courts. The Kleroterion protocol attributes decision rights to jurors having the right skillset and the right incentives to produce a correct decision at the lowest cost and the shortest amount of time.

What are the key applications of Kleroterion?

The Kleroterion protocol is especially suited for decisions that require a high level of transparency. An important use case involves arbitration. In this case, jurors have to analyze evidence and produce a verdict about which party is right in a dispute. Kleroterion is 100% voluntary and all parties have to opt-in (but they can do so before a dispute arises).

How are jurors selected and how do you make sure they do honest work?

Kleroterion uses advanced concepts from game theory, collective intelligence, cryptography and blockchain to make sure that jurors have an incentive to make honest decisions without having to reveal their true identity. The incentive structure is based on a reputation token called pinakion that represents the right of users to be drawn as jurors. Users doing high quality work will earn money over time. Users doing poor quality work or trying to abuse the system will lose money. To learn more about how incentives work, read our white paper.

How does Kleroterion decide the number of jurors that will make a decision in a given case?

The number of jurors is decided by the parties when the contract is signed. If, after the decision, a party is not satisfied (maybe, because it thinks that jurors did not vote honestly), it can appeal and pay to have the decision made again. In the next round, a new decision will be made with double the amount of decision makers.

Can I make money by being a decision-maker in Kleroterion?

Kleroterion needs skilled people to serve as jurors. Jurors earn arbitration fees for their work. The more cases you solve and the best the quality of your work, the more money you can make.

I am a researcher and want to study Kleroterion. Can I do it?

Kleroterion is a new research field in game theory, blockchain, collective intelligence and business. If you’re an academic wishing to study Kleroterion, please contact us.

Can I build on Kleroterion?

Kleroterion is an open source multipurpose decision-making protocol that can be used for making different kinds of decisions. If you’re a developer wanting to build on Kleroterion technology, please contact us.

Who owns Kleroterion?

Kleroterion is an open source project that works as a decentralized autonomous organization on Ethereum. The community is the owner of Kleroterion and decide the evolution of the protocol with a liquid democracy mechanism.
